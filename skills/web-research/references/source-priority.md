# Source Priority

Use this reference to choose which sources should drive the answer.

## Preferred Order

1. Official or primary source
- Vendor documentation
- Government publication
- Maintainer release notes
- Original paper or dataset
- Company investor or newsroom post

2. Reliable secondary source
- Reputable wire services and major news organizations
- High-quality technical publications summarizing official changes

3. Tertiary or community source
- Forums, blogs, aggregators, and discussion threads

## Technical Research

- Prefer official documentation, maintainer changelogs, specifications, and primary repositories.
- Use community posts only to discover leads, not as the final authority, unless no primary source exists.

## Current Events and Public Facts

- Prioritize sources that clearly show when the event happened and when the article was published.
- Cross-check breaking news with at least two reputable sources when possible.

## High-Stakes Topics

For medical, legal, or financial questions:

- Prefer primary sources or regulator guidance first.
- State uncertainty clearly when sources conflict.
- Include the exact date on time-sensitive facts.

## Disallowed Shortcuts

- Do not rely on search snippets without opening the source.
- Do not treat SEO comparison pages as authoritative for pricing or policy.
- Do not present unstated inference as confirmed fact.
